    <h2>Etymology</h2>
    <p>
        From Latin <strong>virtuosus</strong>, from virtus meaning "excellence" or "good character".
    </p>

    <h2>Definition</h2>
    <p>
        A word<strong>virtuou</strong> means having or showing high moral standards.
    </p>

    <h3>Denotation</h3>
    <p>
        In its literal sense, <strong>virtuous</strong> refers to:
    </p>
    <ul>
        <li>Moral goodness</li>
        <li>Ethical behavior</li>
    </ul>

    <div class="example">
        <strong>Example (Denotation):</strong><br>
        He is a virtuous person who follows the rules.
    </div>

    <h3>Connotation</h3>
    <p>
        Beyond its literal meaning, <strong>virtuous</strong> often suggests:
    </p>
    <ul>
        <li>Righteousness</li>
        <li>Being worthy of respect or admiration</li>
    </ul>

    <div class="example">
        <strong>Example (Connotation):</strong><br>
        She lived a virtiuous life devoted to helping others.
    </div>
